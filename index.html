Favorite Tools

Jira Software: I prefer to use it for both professional and personal life. Jira is the most complete and free tool that I have discovered that makes task creation, assignment, and tracking easier. I also plan my weekly tasks in Jira and track them as a sprint.

Emacs: Whenever I am about to make quick edits, I use Emacs. I don't use it believing that it is better than Vim; rather, I was just exposed to Emacs first ;). Currently, I am not skilled enough to use it for large projects and for remote environments. For them, I use VSCode.

FFplay: In the past, whenever I was about to open a video file and have a quick look, I had to load a giant video player. Even the great ones such as VLC would have unnecessary load. But today, ffplay makes it much easier, faster, and lighter. It is also great to view IP cameras and live streams over the network.

Tmux: While working with multiple shells, tmux can be a lifesaver. For instance, if you are working with a docker container, and want multiple simultaneous shells in one container, just install tmux. It also makes pane and window management much easier. Moreover, when you close the terminal app, tmux sessions are preseved in /tmp, and hence can be re-attached.
